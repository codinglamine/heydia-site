Maker · heydia

Hi, I'm Lamine.

I'm a high-school student in Paris building heydia, the assistant I always wished I had: one calm place that actually runs my day instead of adding to it.

I'm in 10th grade at EIB Étoile in Paris, and I'm deep into AI, not just using it, but figuring out how to build with it. heydia started as a personal project: I kept living across a dozen tabs, calendar here, inbox there, notes somewhere else, tasks I'd forget the moment I closed the lid, and every "AI assistant" I tried just talked. None of them actually did anything with the tools I already use.

So I built one that does. heydia reads your real calendar and mail, takes your meeting notes, sorts what matters into a priority matrix every morning, and acts across your apps, privately, on your own AI key, with your data staying yours.

Why I'm building it

Most tools are designed to grab your attention. I wanted the opposite: a quiet layer that handles the busywork so the day has more room in it. Building heydia is how I'm learning what AI is actually good at, where it genuinely helps, where it gets in the way, and how to make something people would want to keep open all day.

It's local-first by heart: your data is yours, never sold, never used to train models. I'm a student, not a data company, heydia only works if you trust it with your day.

Where it's going

Right now heydia runs as a desktop app with a growing set of connectors. Next is the cloud version, sign in from anywhere, pick a plan, bring your own AI key, and more integrations as people request them. If you want to follow along or shape what gets built, get in early.
